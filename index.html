Me, Myself, & Korean Entertainment

About

Hi, my name is Nancy! I created this site to showcase how my love for Korean entertainment has developed over the years, starting from discovering kpop for the first time to how I am involved in it today. Hope you enjoy my story:)

Discovering Kpop

In high school, I came across a Youtube video called Teens React To Exo - Call Me Baby (K-pop). The teens had a positive reaction to the song which got me interested in watching the MV myself. I had never seen a genre of music that made the choreography an essential part of a song and so ... watching these 12 guys dancing so in sync with each other was a pretty cool thing to discover. On top of that the music seemed very different than what I was used to with American genres. As you can imagine, I started watching and searching for Exo's other songs and fell into a deep hole of obsession. But it wasn't only their songs that was so attention grabbing; I later found that they had really fun and diverse personalities while watching their live Korean TV specials. Pretty soon I became a true fan, waiting for their upcoming new song releases every few months called "comebacks" and squealing from excitement whenever my favorite member came on screen. Don't laugh, I was only 15. Mind you, I had never really cared about any American boy band before, so me getting all excited over a Korean boy group was out of my character. I've been a fan of EXO and many other kpop groups ever since.

My Kpop Dancing Career

HS and Home

I first started to learn how to dance in HS after having watched so many online kpop choreographies that seemed so fun. In secret, when noone was home, I would loudly play kpop music and attempt to mimic the moves that I saw on screen. I didn't progress that much in HS but I had the best time learning how to groove with the music.

College

In college, I auditioned for a variety of Kpop dance clubs. I was obviously still very new to dance, but I got into a few of them and learned a lot from being on teams. After 3 years of dancing, I was finally able to move in a way that didn't look I was having a seizure.

Post Grad

As a post-graduate, I still dance from time to time and continue to help out my dance team as a dance assistant. I teach simple choreography and give feedback to my team members, helping the newbies build confidence in dancing and showing them how fun Kpop dancing can be.
